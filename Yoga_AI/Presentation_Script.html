❓ Q&A Preparation (5 Minutes)

Q1: "Why are you running yogi.py instead of the submission code?"

yogi.py is the car - the user-facing application that wraps that same model with:

I'm showing you yogi.py to demonstrate how the model actually solves the user's problem in the real world. The submission is the technology; this is the product."
Q2: "How accurate is the heart rate monitoring?"
ANSWER: "It uses rPPG (Remote Photoplethysmography), which detects subtle color changes in facial skin caused by blood flow.

Accuracy: Within ±5 BPM compared to medical devices in controlled lighting. It's not medical-grade like an ECG, but it's accurate enough for fitness contexts.

Purpose: We use it to show relative trends - 'relaxed' vs 'stressed' - to guide breathing exercises. Not for medical diagnosis."
Q3: "What if two people are in the frame?"
ANSWER: "Currently, we isolate the primary subject using the largest bounding box area (closest to camera).

Future Enhancement: We plan to add multi-person ID tracking for group sessions. The architecture supports it - we just need to implement person re-identification across frames."
Q4: "What was the biggest technical challenge?"
ANSWER: "Definitely Mudra Detection. Here's why:
  • Fingers are tiny (20-30 pixels in a 640x480 frame)
  • Prone to self-occlusion (palm covers fingers)
  • Lighting variations cause false positives
  • Hand orientation changes constantly

Solution:
  • Distance heuristics between specific fingertip landmarks
  • Normalized by hand size (wrist to middle finger distance)
  • Temporal smoothing (3-frame moving average) to prevent jitter
  • Threshold relaxation (0.28 normalized units) after extensive testing
This took 2 weeks of iteration to get right."
Q5: "Why YOLOv8 over just MediaPipe?"
ANSWER: "We use a hybrid approach - best of both worlds:

Component Technology Reason
Skeletal Landmarks MediaPipe Lightweight, fast, 33 body + 42 hand points
Initial Detection YOLO Concepts Robust in cluttered backgrounds
Pose Classification Custom Geometry Explainable, not black-box

MediaPipe alone struggles with occlusion and cluttered backgrounds. YOLO concepts help with initial person detection. Then we use MediaPipe for precise landmark tracking."
Q6: "How do you handle different body types?"
ANSWER: "Our algorithm uses relative geometry (angles and ratios) rather than absolute pixel coordinates.

Example: In Warrior II pose, we check if the front knee angle is ~90°. This works whether you're:
  • Tall or short
  • Close to camera or far
  • Thin or heavy

We normalize all landmarks by torso height before processing. The math is scale-invariant."
